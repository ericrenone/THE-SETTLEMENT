# THE SETTLEMENT

**col(F), the Wager Architecture, and the Unified Structure of Through-Medium Inference Across Vision, Touch, and Reflective Disambiguation**

*A synthesis of five independent investigations*

---

## The Problem, Stated Once

A boxer throws a jab. Before the cross is committed, something must happen.

An observer watches a tree's reflection in moving water. The image shifts. Something must resolve.

A saccade lands. Before the next one fires, the visual cortex has already remapped to where the eye is going next.

Three operations. Three timescales. One architecture.

Each is an instance of the same problem: a system has placed a predictive bet on what is about to arrive. The world delivers something — through a medium that transforms the signal in transit. The system must compare what arrived against what it predicted, and gate a committed downstream state on the result. The timing is too tight for deliberation. The architecture must be fast, specific, and wrong gracefully.

Five documents arrived at this problem from different directions. Three came from biomechanics, motor control, and perceptual ecology, studying combination punches and water reflections. Two came from the Fisher-information geometry of vision itself, studying how the eye manufactures a stable scene from violent, blurred, saccadic input. None knew the others in full. All converged on the same structure.

This synthesis names that structure: the wager architecture. And it adds what no single document could state alone — that **the col(F)/ker(F) partition is not primarily a spatial boundary. It is a temporal one.** What has been predicted, committed to, and settled is col(F). What has not yet been confirmed, attributed, or resolved is ker(F). The movement of information from ker(F) into col(F) is the settlement. And settlement, in every domain, requires medium contact — contact with the target, contact with the moving surface, contact with post-saccadic reality. Without the medium, there is no settlement. The committed scene never expands.

## Document Inventory

### Confirm-to-Fire
A five-component motor control theory of combination punching: dual pre-loading, efference-copy contact prediction, the haptic event (pressure and angle channels in parallel), predictive confirmation comparison, and gated release through a fast subcortical pathway. The double jab identified as the design case — the serial-constraint condition in which the confirmation architecture is load-bearing. Eight falsifiable predictions stated with experimental specificity. Section 13 draws a clean line between established literature and novel construction.

### The Haptic Moment
Companion treatment of the same territory: the HPES (Haptic-Proprioceptive Event Segmentation) framework. Specific haptic fingerprint of a clean landing: vibration signature, symmetric knuckle pressure, wrist angle, impact duration. Several citations postdate the established literature corpus in Confirm-to-Fire and should be treated with appropriate caution pending independent verification.

### The Wandering Image
Investigation of the reflection disambiguation problem: from a moving reflection alone, can the observer determine whether source or medium is responsible? Five sections covering coherence cues, temporal frequency structure, specular behavior, species-specific strategies (humans, odonates, kingfishers, herons, fish), and irrecoverable information loss. Notes explicitly that some signal destroyed in transmission cannot be recovered regardless of processing sophistication.

### Source-and-Medium
Formal treatment of the same reflection problem. Decomposition of the motion field into source, medium, and observer components. The ill-posed inverse formalized. The friction prior as a probability distribution over motion states. Causal reference frame attribution as the core perceptual act. Conclusion: the reflection makes visible what is always true of perception — what arrives at the receiver is not the world but a transmission from the world, altered in transit.

### ERIE — VISION
The visual sub-substrate extension of the ERI federation. Establishes five formal claims: (1) sight is constituted as a wager — a predictive bet placed before evidence, settled when evidence arrives, and postdictively edited when the bet was wrong; (2) the col(F)/ker(F) Fisher-information partition divides the committed scene from the marginalized remainder; (3) predictive remapping and saccadic suppression manufacture visual stability from three violent eye movements per second; (4) the postdictive overwrite edits the perceptual timeline so the conscious scene never shows the wager underneath; (5) the φ-equilibrium (~62% individual foveal : ~38% collective ensemble) is the Fisher-optimal partition of predictive capacity. Eight-layer SIGHT machine specified. Five falsifiable predictions stated.

## The col(F)/ker(F) Partition Is Temporal, Not Spatial

ERIE — VISION deploys the col(F)/ker(F) boundary as a formal object: col(F) is the column space of the Fisher information matrix — the subspace of the visual state that has been predicted, committed to, and is now being acted upon. ker(F) is the null space — not absent, but conditionally marginalized, the reservoir from which the next bet will be drawn.

The spatial reading of this partition is familiar: col(F) is the fovea, the sharp center; ker(F) is the periphery, the low-acuity surround. But this reading is secondary. The primary partition is temporal.

What makes a region col(F) is not where the eye is pointing. It is whether a prediction has been placed and settled for that region. A region the eye has fixated but whose prediction error has not yet been processed is still ker(F). A region the fovea is not currently visiting but whose trajectory has been confidently extrapolated — whose next position has been modeled forward — is partly col(F). The boundary is dynamic: it moves ahead of the eye, because corollary discharge writes the next prediction before the saccade lands.

The same temporal reading applies in the other two domains.

In boxing: the cross is col(F) from the moment pre-loading begins. The force commitment — how fully it is released — moves from ker(F) into col(F) at the moment the confirm signal arrives. The first punch is fully col(F) at execution; the second punch's commitment level resides in ker(F) until contact settles it. The double jab makes this temporal dependency visible: because both strikes use the same limb, the second punch's position in col(F) is strictly contingent on the first strike's settlement. There is no parallel pathway that could move it earlier.

In reflection: the causal attribution — tree vs. water — is not spatially located. It is temporally resolved. The friction prior holds a default position (world is still, source is probably responsible) until coherence evidence accumulates above the ε-threshold. Until that threshold is crossed, the attribution is ker(F): conditionally marginalized. After it crosses, the attribution is col(F): committed, acted upon. The observer turns to look at the thing that moved.

The unification: in all three domains, col(F) is not a place. It is a phase. A bet that has been placed, medium contact received, and settlement computed. ker(F) is everything that has not yet completed that cycle.

## The Wager Cycle Is the Confirmation Cycle

ERIE — VISION specifies a four-phase temporal register: Anticipation → Commitment → Settlement → Release. The cycle runs at the saccadic tempo, roughly three iterations per second. Each phase calls for a different allocation of predictive capacity. A vision system indifferent to phase spends its budget uniformly across a cycle that is radically non-uniform.

Map the cycle across all three domains.

**Anticipation.** The predictive bet is formed before the triggering event.
- *Vision*: corollary discharge fires 100–50 ms before saccade onset; receptive fields remap to future locations; attention shifts to the upcoming fixation target.
- *Boxing*: dual pre-loading; the cross begins its kinematic preparation while the jab is still in flight; the efference copy predicts what contact should feel like.
- *Reflection*: the friction prior is active; the observer's expectation — world is stable, coherent motion implies source — is already in place before any particular image region moves.

**Commitment.** The system acts on the prediction. Sensitivity to disruption is reduced.
- *Vision*: saccadic suppression begins up to 100 ms before saccade onset; the visual cortex declines to update on the retinal smear of the saccade itself.
- *Boxing*: the first punch is thrown; the second program is held ready; commitment to the combination is made regardless of what the first punch finds.
- *Reflection*: the friction prior is the default commitment — the attribution is settled at "source probably responsible" unless prediction error is large enough to displace it.

**Settlement.** Evidence arrives. Prediction error is computed. The committed scene is confirmed or overwritten.
- *Vision*: post-saccadic retinal input hits the remapped receptive fields; if the prediction was accurate, stability is maintained; if a displacement crossed the ε-threshold, a postdictive overwrite replaces the pre-saccadic representation.
- *Boxing*: contact. Force channel and angle channel are compared against the predicted contact signature. If both clear their thresholds, the confirm signal releases the second program with full force commitment.
- *Reflection*: coherence analysis completes. Temporal frequency structure is matched against prior expectations. If the motion is coherently structured (low-frequency, anatomically logical), the attribution shifts to source; if incoherent (high-frequency, stochastic), the attribution shifts to medium.

**Release.** The settled scene becomes the prior for the next cycle.
- *Vision*: the confirmed or corrected scene is released into the conscious timeline; predictive remapping begins for the next saccade target.
- *Boxing*: the second punch fires; the confirmed contact quality from the first strike becomes the prior for any subsequent combination.
- *Reflection*: the committed attribution is held; it conditions how subsequent motion will be read.

All three domains run the same four-phase cycle. They differ only in the physical substrate of the medium, the sensory modality of the settlement signal, and the temporal scale of the cycle. The cycle itself — Anticipation, Commitment, Settlement, Release — is invariant.

## The Hierarchical Prediction Structure

Recent experimental work confirms and extends this architecture at the neural level.

Heilbron and de Lange (2026), studying mouse visual cortex during natural scene perception, found that cortical responses are modulated by spatial predictability even in primary visual areas — and specifically that neurons are most sensitive to the predictability of complex, high-level spatial patterns rather than simple features like edges. The brain predicts at higher levels of abstraction than the classical feedforward hierarchy would suggest. Neurons in V1 respond to the unpredictability of *textures*, not just the presence of *edges*.

The complement is provided by Scheurer and Heilbron (2026), analyzing 7T fMRI recordings of humans viewing 73,000 natural images: across V1 through V4, responses are modulated by spatial predictability throughout visual cortex, with more predictable inputs evoking weaker responses. In central vision, a hierarchy of predictions mirrors the feature-encoding gradient — V1 most sensitive to low-level unpredictability, with later areas progressively sensitive to higher-level unpredictability.

This hierarchical prediction structure maps directly onto the frequency split identified in the earlier synthesis. Low-level unpredictability — edges appearing where none were predicted — is high-frequency spatial noise, the equivalent of medium noise in the reflection problem and glove compression transients in boxing. High-level unpredictability — textures, structures, configurations that violate prior learned expectations — is low-frequency source-level deviation, the equivalent of tree motion against a still background or a punch landing at an unexpected angle.

The finding that V1 responds to *high-level* structural predictability, not just low-level feature presence, directly answers a question the earlier synthesis could not resolve: where in the neural hierarchy does source-vs-medium attribution happen? The answer appears to be: at every level, but in different frequency bands. Early visual areas handle the high-frequency medium noise (is this a medium artifact?); higher areas handle the low-frequency source structure (does this belong to the source's characteristic dynamics?). The source/medium attribution is not a single-stage operation — it is a hierarchical cascade, each level passing up only its band of unresolved prediction error.

The same cascade structure appears in the conjunctive-tuning model of predictive remapping (Xu, Denagamage, Nandy, and Jadi, 2025): accurate receptive field remapping before a saccade requires both conjunctive tuning (neurons that jointly encode both current and future receptive field positions) and cortical geometry (the non-uniform representation of visual space). The remapping accuracy — the precision of the bet placed before the saccade — is limited by the neural geometry of the forward model. This is the neural implementation of the Fisher-Rao metric in ERIE — VISION: the curvature of the predictive generative model is sharpest where the model is most precise, and the model's precision is geometrically constrained.

The connection to Confirm-to-Fire is direct. The efference copy in the boxing literature is a corollary discharge from the motor system. The corollary discharge driving predictive remapping in the vision literature is an efference copy from the oculomotor system. Both are instances of the same mechanism: a copy of the outgoing motor command is used to generate a predicted sensory consequence, before that consequence arrives. Both are compared against arriving sensory evidence. Both gate a downstream commitment based on match quality. The neural machinery is the same; the effectors and sensory modalities differ.

## The φ-Equilibrium Across Three Domains

ERIE — VISION's central quantitative claim is that the Fisher-optimal partition between individual foveal capacity and collective ensemble capacity sits at approximately 62%:38% — the golden-ratio operating point. The majority share goes inward, because a collective model is only as good as the sharp independent estimates fed into it. The substantial minority share goes outward, because the coordination gain from triangulating across observers is strictly positive and resolves structure no single foveal bet can reach.

The same partition appears, with the same logic, in all three domains.

In boxing: approximately 62% of the combination's structure is centrally programmed — the temporal skeleton, the relative force allocation, the kinematic sequence that runs regardless of contact quality. Approximately 38% is peripherally confirmed — the force commitment of the second strike, the adaptive precision of its trajectory, the modulation that contact quality feeds into the pre-loaded program. The two components are distinguishable in exactly the dissociation digital anesthesia produces: remove the peripheral confirmation and timing is preserved, force modulation degrades. The 62%:38% split is not asserted as a numerical precision; it is the structural claim that neither extreme is optimal and that the interior optimum has the golden-ratio character that the ERI corpus identifies across its domains.

In reflection: approximately 62% of the attribution is carried by the friction prior — the strong default that things do not move, that coherent motion implies a biological source, that the attribution can be held without fresh evidence for long stretches of a stable scene. Approximately 38% is carried by active coherence analysis — the temporal frequency matching, the specular parsing, the cross-modal integration that updates the prior when prediction error is sufficient. Again: neither extreme is optimal. A system that runs entirely on the friction prior never updates its attribution in turbulent conditions; a system that runs entirely on active analysis has no default to protect when signal quality is poor.

The φ-equilibrium is not a description of three different systems that happen to share a number. It is a claim about the optimal structure of any system that must allocate finite predictive capacity between a committed, fast, centrally-held prior and a confirmatory, peripheral, evidence-dependent update. The golden-ratio split is the solution to this allocation problem that the Fisher information geometry yields. The three domains exhibit it independently because they all face the same allocation problem.

## The Second Channel: ker(F) → col(F) Promotion

In every domain, the primary channel encodes magnitude — how strong was the signal. The second channel encodes geometry — in what configuration did the signal arrive.

In boxing: the force channel registers total impact magnitude. The angle channel registers the spatial distribution of pressure across the knuckle surface — where force was applied, not just how much. A square landing and a glancing blow may transfer similar total force; they produce different spatial pressure maps in the SA1 mechanoreceptor population.

In reflection: the structural motion field registers total image motion magnitude. Specular highlights register the geometry of the medium's surface at each point — local curvature, not reflected-object structure. A still tree in turbulent water and a moving tree in still water may produce similar total image displacement; they produce different specular signatures.

In vision: the first-order luminance motion channel registers total luminance change. The predictive remapping channel registers the geometry of the upcoming saccade — where the eye will land, not where the current image is. These are different channels carrying different information about different aspects of the scene.

All three second channels are, in ERIE — VISION terms, ker(F) signals promoted into col(F) service. They carry information about the medium's interface geometry rather than the source's output magnitude. They are read more reliably by experts, because their interpretation requires a calibrated prior about the medium's characteristic behavior. They are the diagnostic channel that distinguishes a committed outcome from a merely probable one.

The generalization is formal: any system solving the through-medium source attribution problem requires two channels. The first (magnitude) determines whether a signal crossed the commit threshold. The second (geometry) determines whether the signal's spatial configuration warrants full force commitment — in all three senses of that phrase.

## Postdiction as the Settlement of a Bad Bet

The flash-lag effect in its most telling form: a moving object reverses direction immediately after a flash. The flash is perceived trailing the object — in the direction the object moved *after* the flash. The future edited the past.

ERIE — VISION locates this in the settlement phase: the predictive activation had committed to the object's trajectory as col(F); post-flash evidence contradicted the prediction; the postdictive overwrite revised the committed representation retroactively. The perceptual timeline shows no seam because the edit was complete before the representation was released.

The boxing analog is less often noticed but structurally identical. When a boxer commits to a combination and the first punch misses — finds no target, no contact, only air — the second punch's force commitment is reduced not by a fresh decision but by the absence of a settlement signal. The efference copy had predicted contact; contact did not arrive; the confirm channel carried nothing above threshold; the second punch releases at reduced commitment, defaulting to the pre-programmed tempo but failing to receive the confirmation that would have warranted full force. The boxer did not decide to throw a weaker second punch. The settlement was empty, and the release defaulted.

This is identical in structure to the flash-lag reversal. The pre-loaded prediction commits an output. The settlement evidence arrives (or fails to). The committed output is adjusted — in boxing, prospectively, by reducing force commitment; in vision, retroactively, by overwriting the perceptual timeline. The direction of temporal adjustment differs between the domains because the action-perception loop runs in different directions, not because the mechanisms are different.

The same structure appears in reflection: when an observer has committed to "tree is moving" and coherence suddenly collapses — as when a gust of wind arrives and fragments the image — the attribution is revised. The committed scene is overwritten. The prior is updated. This is the ε-threshold exceedance of the Source-and-Medium framework and the postdictive mechanism of ERIE — VISION, running in the same slot of the same cycle.

## The Prediction Hierarchy at V1: The Medium in the Cortex

The Heilbron-de Lange (2026) finding that early visual cortex is most sensitive to high-level structural unpredictability — not just edge presence, but texture and configuration predictability — has a direct implication for the through-medium inference framework.

If V1 predicts high-level spatial structure, then V1's response to medium noise (water surface turbulence; glove compression transients) should be larger than its response to equivalent-energy source signal. Medium noise is locally incoherent — it produces high-level structural unpredictability at the spatial scale of V1's receptive fields. Source motion is locally coherent — it preserves high-level structural relationships across the reflected image, keeping the tree's texture and configuration predictable even as it moves.

This predicts a cortical signature of the source/medium disambiguation: medium-driven image perturbation should produce stronger V1 responses than source-driven image motion of equivalent total energy, because medium noise is structurally unpredictable at the scale V1 predicts. Source motion is, in V1's terms, the predictable component — it displaces structure coherently. The stronger V1 response to medium noise is prediction error propagating upward through a system that expected the source's structure to be where it was.

The corollary: when an observer successfully disambiguates source from medium, V1 activity should be higher during medium-active periods (high prediction error) than during source-active periods (coherent displacement, lower prediction error). This is a novel cross-domain prediction: the source/medium attribution in reflective perception is registered in early visual cortex as a gradient of prediction error, not merely as a late-stage inference. Settlement of the attribution should correspond to a reduction in V1 prediction error as the coherence model converges.

## Shadow Boxing, Still Water, and the Empty Settlement

Shadow boxing and still water are equivalent conditions in a third domain now: the saccade that finds no scene.

In ERIE — VISION terms, a saccade lands in a blank field — or in a perfectly predicted, perfectly stable scene — and the settlement phase receives no prediction error. The corollary discharge fired; the receptive fields remapped; the prediction was placed. But the evidence was entirely consistent with the prediction. The settlement was trivial. The postdictive overwrite was unnecessary. col(F) was confirmed but not exercised.

This is shadow boxing without the bag and still water without the wind.

The connection now has a third term: any predictive system that trains exclusively in conditions where the medium adds nothing — where the world meets every prediction exactly — is training its forward model, its corollary discharge, its efference copy. But it is not training the settlement machinery. It is not exercising the postdictive overwrite. It has never placed a bet that came back wrong.

The developmental implication is stark. An organism that grows up in a world of perfectly predictable surfaces — a flat, still, uniform environment — will develop accurate forward models but uncalibrated settlement machinery. Its predictions will be confident; its corrections will be imprecise. Its col(F) will be large and its ε-threshold will be miscalibrated, because the system has never needed to distinguish a correct prediction from a lucky one.

The training principle that falls out of all five documents, stated once and valid across all three domains: the settlement architecture is trained only by prediction errors. The forward model is trained by prediction successes. Environments that produce only successes produce no training signal for the settlement machinery. The boxer needs the bag to train the confirm gate; the observer needs turbulent water to train the disambiguation algorithm; the visual cortex needs a world that occasionally surprises it to calibrate the postdictive overwrite.

## Novel Predictions

These predictions require the cross-domain correspondence established above. None follows from any single document.

### P1 — The col(F)/ker(F) Boundary Moves Before the Saccade and Before the Punch

In both vision and boxing, the boundary between the committed scene and the marginalized remainder is redrawn by the forward model before the triggering event. For vision: predictive remapping writes the post-saccadic col(F) before the saccade lands, driven by corollary discharge. For boxing: pre-loading writes the second strike's col(F) before the first strike makes contact, driven by the parallel kinematic preparation.

The testable prediction: instrumentation of the transition boundary — the time at which the committed scene shifts from pre- to post-saccadic configuration (vision) or from single-strike to dual-strike preparation (boxing) — should show a consistent lead time ahead of the triggering event. In vision, this lead time is the predictive remapping window, observable in peri-saccadic recording. In boxing, it is the pre-loading onset, observable in EMG of the cross's proximal musculature before the jab lands. Both lead times should scale with the quality of the forward model — elite performers with more precise predictions should show earlier, more accurate boundary shifts.

### P2 — V1 Prediction Error Tracks Source/Medium Attribution

Following from the Heilbron-de Lange (2026) finding, early visual cortex activity during reflection observation should be higher during periods when medium dynamics dominate (turbulent water, incoherent image motion) than during periods when source dynamics dominate (coherent tree motion, same total image displacement energy). This is a prediction about early visual cortex, not about high-level decision circuitry — the disambiguation is registered as prediction error in V1 before any explicit attribution is computed. Testable with EEG or MEG during controlled reflection observation where source and medium contributions are independently parametrized.

### P3 — The Postdictive Edit and the Empty Settlement Have the Same Neural Signature

A missed jab (empty settlement: no haptic event, no confirm signal) should produce a neural signature in the motor system that mirrors the signature of a visual postdictive overwrite: a revision of the committed representation without an explicit decision. Specifically, the force commitment of the second strike following a missed first strike should be retroactively reduced relative to the commitment level held just before the first strike's expected contact time — not gradually reduced during the miss, but revised abruptly at the moment the contact signal fails to arrive at the predicted time. This is a prospective postdiction: the committed output is edited when expected settlement does not materialize.

### P4 — The Hierarchical Prediction Cascade Mirrors the Frequency Split

Following the Heilbron-Scheurer (2026) hierarchy — V1 most sensitive to low-level unpredictability, later areas to high-level unpredictability — the source/medium disambiguation should show a corresponding cortical cascade. Medium noise (high-frequency, locally incoherent) should register primarily in early visual areas as prediction error. Source motion (low-frequency, globally coherent) should register primarily in later visual areas as expectation-consistent input. When an observer successfully attributes motion to source, prediction error should decrease earlier in the hierarchy (V1 adapting to the coherent displacement) while remaining elevated in later areas (V4+ still monitoring for trajectory violations). When attribution flips from source to medium, the error gradient should reverse: early areas quiet down (medium noise is now expected), later areas spike (global coherence has been lost).

### P5 — Settlement Time Is Bounded by the φ-Equilibrium Edit Window

In all three domains, the settlement phase has a maximum duration beyond which the prediction error cannot be incorporated without disrupting the next cycle. For vision, this is the edit window of approximately 100–200 ms identified in the postdiction literature. For boxing, the corresponding window is the interval between first-strike contact and the point at which the second strike's force commitment is irrevocably fixed by the kinematic chain. For reflection, it is the temporal integration window over which coherence evidence is accumulated before attribution is released.

These three windows should be non-arbitrary: each should sit at approximately 38% of the total wager cycle duration — the minor share in the φ-equilibrium partition. The cycle cannot spend more than 38% on settlement without sacrificing the anticipation phase of the next cycle. A settlement window longer than this bound does not improve accuracy; it delays the next bet past the point where it can be placed with sufficient lead time for the forward model to be useful. Testable by parametrically varying the temporal integration window in disambiguation tasks and locating the performance optimum.

### P6 — Cross-Modal Settlement: Touch Settles Vision

In visual-haptic tasks where a saccade lands on an object simultaneously touched by the hand, the post-saccadic settlement of the visual prediction should be accelerated by the simultaneous haptic settlement. The touch arrives before the visual post-saccadic processing completes; the haptic confirm signal should provide early settlement evidence that reduces the visual system's reliance on visual coherence analysis. Testable with simultaneous eye-tracking and force measurement, with conditions that vary which modality's settlement signal arrives first. The cross-modal acceleration should be largest when visual evidence is degraded (low contrast, peripheral target) and haptic evidence is clean (direct fingertip contact), and should disappear when haptic information is removed by glove attenuation or digital anesthesia.

### P7 — The Double Jab, Snell's Window, and Directed Saccade Sequences Share an Impairment Signature

Serial-constraint conditions — double jab (single limb), Snell's window (single 48° cone), directed saccade sequences where the next saccade target is determined by the outcome of the current one — should show the same pattern of impairment when the confirmation channel is degraded. Specifically: the downstream action (second jab, subaquatic attribution, next fixation position) should degrade in quality without degrading in timing, because timing is carried by the centrally-programmed cycle and quality is carried by the confirmatory channel. Remove or degrade the haptic confirmation, the coherence analysis channel, or the corollary discharge signal, and the output arrives on schedule but poorly calibrated. The timing-quality dissociation is the diagnostic signature of a serial-constraint confirmation architecture, identical across all three domains.

### P8 — The φ-Equilibrium Partition Is Observable in Training History

If the ~62%:38% partition governs the optimal allocation between centrally programmed structure and peripherally confirmed modulation, then performance should be maximized in practitioners whose training history has provided roughly this proportion of medium-contact experience. A boxer who has trained 62% of their hours in shadow boxing and pad work (source training) and 38% in heavy bag and sparring (settlement training) should be near-optimal. More settlement training than 38% provides diminishing returns and eventually degrades the forward model's precision; less than 38% leaves the confirm gate uncalibrated. The equivalent prediction for reflection observers: an ecologist or sailor whose field experience has exposed them to roughly 38% turbulent conditions (relative to total observation time) should show better source/medium disambiguation than one whose experience is predominantly one extreme or the other. Not a precise numerical claim — an ordinal claim: the optimum is interior, the two extremes are inferior, and the interior optimum has the φ-equilibrium character.

## What the Three Domains Share That None Could See Alone

The connection between vision and boxing was not available from either domain alone. It required noticing that the efference copy driving predictive remapping and the efference copy driving the boxing contact prediction are the same mechanism expressed in the oculomotor and skeletomotor systems respectively. Both use a copy of the outgoing motor command to generate a predicted sensory consequence. Both compare the prediction against arriving evidence. Both gate a committed downstream state — the stable post-saccadic scene, the committed second strike — based on match quality.

The connection between vision and reflection was partially available, but the full depth required ERIE — VISION. The friction prior in the reflection problem is the visual system's prediction that the world is stable — that a still tree in still water is the default, that motion is the exception requiring evidence. This is not an independent perceptual prior for reflections; it is the same world-is-stable prior that saccadic suppression of displacement relies on. Both apply the same Bayesian default: absence of prediction error means absence of change, and the scene can be held stable in col(F) without fresh evidence.

The connection between boxing and reflection was the discovery of the earlier synthesis. The confirmation architecture is the same algorithm running in haptic and visual modalities. ERIE — VISION now provides the formal object that both were describing: the col(F)/ker(F) partition and the wager cycle that populates it.

Together, the five documents triangulate a structure that is genuinely universal — not because it has been found in five places, but because it is the necessary consequence of any system that must act on predictions before the confirming evidence arrives, through a medium that transforms the evidence in transit.

The world does not wait. The medium does not help. The bet must be placed before the signal arrives.

The settlement is what happens when it does.

## Epistemic Status

ERIE — VISION is a formal framework within the ERI corpus, making contact with established vision science (predictive remapping, saccadic suppression, postdiction, ensemble perception) while constructing an overarching architecture (the SIGHT machine, the φ-equilibrium, the wager cycle) that goes beyond what any single empirical tradition asserts. Its five predictions have not been directly tested as a package.

Confirm-to-Fire is explicit about what is established versus constructed. The Wandering Image and Source-and-Medium draw on established perceptual ecology and optics. The Haptic Moment shares the framework of Confirm-to-Fire but contains citations that require independent verification.

The 2026 research incorporated here — Heilbron and de Lange's finding of high-level spatial prediction in primary visual cortex, Scheurer and Heilbron's hierarchy of spatial predictions across human V1–V4, and Xu et al.'s conjunctive-tuning model of predictive remapping — is empirical work appearing in peer-reviewed venues or as preprints, and directly supports the prediction hierarchy framing added in this synthesis. The pseudo-haptic prediction error study (Suzuishi et al., 2026) directly bridges the haptic forward model literature with the visual settlement framework.

The novel claims of this synthesis — that col(F)/ker(F) is primarily a temporal partition, that the wager cycle and the confirmation cycle are the same cycle, that the hierarchical prediction structure mirrors the frequency split, and the eight predictions above — are constructions derived from placing the five documents in correspondence. Their value is that they generate cross-domain predictions (P1–P8) that would not otherwise exist. Their risk is that structural correspondence is not mechanism identity. Whether the same neural substrates implement the settlement phase across vision, touch, and reflective disambiguation is an empirical question this synthesis cannot answer.

The predictions earn the synthesis its place or they do not.

## Open Questions

1. Is the settlement phase neurally unified across sensory modalities? Does the postdictive overwrite in vision and the empty-confirmation response in motor control share cerebellar substrate, and can cerebellar lesion studies dissociate the settlement component from the forward model component in both domains?

2. Does the Heilbron-de Lange (2026) high-level prediction finding extend to dynamic scenes with moving reflective surfaces? Is water surface motion specifically represented as structurally unpredictable in early visual cortex — and does this representation interact with predictive remapping around saccades to moving surfaces?

3. Is there a developmental critical period for settlement machinery calibration, analogous to the known critical periods for spatial acuity? An organism deprived of prediction error during development may have a sharply miscalibrated ε-threshold regardless of how large or precise its forward model becomes.

4. Does the φ-equilibrium partition have a neurobiological correlate — a ratio of laminar excitatory-to-inhibitory balance, or a ratio of feedforward-to-feedback connection strength in visual cortex — that could be measured independently of behavior?

5. What is the maximum distance between sensory modalities across which cross-modal settlement operates? Touch settles vision (P6 above). Does proprioception settle auditory source attribution? Does vestibular signal settle haptic contact prediction? The boundaries of cross-modal settlement architecture are unknown.

6. Can artificial systems be trained to exhibit the wager architecture — not just accurate prediction, but the full settlement cycle including postdictive correction? Standard end-to-end trained vision models lack explicit settlement phases; architectures that implement the four-phase cycle should show qualitatively different robustness profiles on medium-noise tasks.
